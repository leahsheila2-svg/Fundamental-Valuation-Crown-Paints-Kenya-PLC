## Project Overview

This project focuses on performing a fundamental valuation of Crown Paints Plc using financial modeling techniques in Excel.
The objective is to estimate the intrinsic value of the company by analyzing its financial performance, forecasting future cash flows, and applying appropriate valuation methodologies.It demonstrates how financial data can be structured, analyzed, and translated into investment insights.

## Investment Objective

The objective of this project is to:
- Estimate the intrinsic value of Crown Paints Kenya PLC.
- Compare valuation outputs across different methodologies.
- Assess whether the stock is overvalued or undervalued.
- Formulate a data driven investment recommendation.

 ## Tools & Techniques
- Ms Excel (Financial Modeling FAST standard)
- Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
- Relative Valuation:
- Price-to-Earnings (P/E)
- EV/EBITDA
- Price-to-Sales (P/S)
- Financial Statement Analysis

## Model Structure
The valuation model is organized into key analytical layers:

1. Historical Financial Analysis: Review of revenue, profitability, and cost structure to establish baseline performance trends.

2. Assumptions & Key Drivers: Forward looking assumptions including growth rates, margins, and valuation multiples.

3. Forecasting: Projection of financial performance to support valuation calculations.

4. Valuation Layer: Application of multiple valuation techniques to estimate fair value.

## Valuation Methodologies Explained

To ensure a well rounded analysis, the model applies four valuation approaches:

1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
a) Concept: DCF estimates the intrinsic value of a company based on the present value of its future cash flows.
b) Essence: Focuses on the company’s ability to generate cash & Considers time value of money.
c) Fair Value: KES 59.00 that Indicates the stock is close to fairly valued, with minimal downside (-1.7%)

2. Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Multiple
a) Concept: Values the company based on its earnings relative to comparable companies or market benchmarks.
b) Essence: Reflects how much investors are willing to pay per unit of earnings, Market sentiment driven & Sensitive to profitability.
c) Fair Value: KES 34.39, thus suggests the stock is significantly overvalued.

4. EV/EBITDA Multiple
a) Concept: Values the firm based on operating performance, independent to the capital structure.
b) Essence: it removes financing and accounting distortions which leds to focuses on core operations
c) Fair Value: KES 44.81 this indicates downside risk and overvaluation

6. Price-to-Sales (P/S) Multiple
a) Concept: Values the company based on revenue rather than profit.
b) Essence: Is that it focuses on top line performance & its less sensitive to accounting variations
c) Fair Value: KES 56.90 it is slightly below market price → mild overvaluation.

## Valuation Summary
- Current Market Price: KES 60.00

## Investment Interpretation
The valuation presents a clear divergence:
- The DCF model suggests the company is fairly valued
- Relative valuation methods consistently indicate overvaluation
- This implies that current market pricing may reflects optimistic growth of the organization & the blended valuation highlights limited upside and potential downside risk.

# Investment Recommendation: HOLD
- Intrinsic value supports current pricing levels
- Relative valuation signals downside risk (~18.7%)

The stock is best positioned as a HOLD, balancing fair value with valuation caution.
